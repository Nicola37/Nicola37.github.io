About Me
My name is Nicola Frachesen, and I am a user experience designer, programmer, and writer from Miami, Florida.

I have a BS in Digital Arts and Sciences, a computer science degree with additional art and design elements, from the University of Florida.

I enjoy reading, playing video games, and being intensely curious about pretty much everything.
My Work
Dying Dreams
My senior project, Dying Dreams, is a video game with no video; a completely audio-based role-playing game designed to be inclusive of the visually impaired. It was made in Unity, is 3 to 4 hours long, and includes voice work primarily from actor Yan Bergquist. My paper on it can be found here.
Donator
Group project from my UX design class made for a client from the Royal Bank of Canada. We designed, wireframed, and presented an app for donating to the homeless. It was created using Invision. Our presentation poster can be found here.
Pixel Story
A pixel art, animation, and gif creator with a focus on customization and storytelling. Created in Java. More information can be found here.
Writing
I greatly enjoy writing in general, and fiction in particular. In addition to having taken writing courses, I have written scripts for several video game projects, including Dying Dreams, and have written a number of short stories. A brief sample of my work can be found here.
